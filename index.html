covAID

A project created by four undergrads trying to hack together from three different time zones

From November 7 to 14th (2020), I participated in a virtual hackathon hosted by Yale University (YHacks). After messaging dozens of people in the event's Slack, I found a team with similar learning goals and expectations. That's when four undergrads dispersed across North America set out to create a COVID-19 related hack. 

Our web application covAID is intended for use between volunteers and organizations to seamlessly coordinate shifts for thing like grocery running, mask making, and transporting immunocompromised peoples. The whole scope of our project was to create sections like a user sign-up form, a scheduling system, and a postings page. While we didn't get to complete each section within a week, we did get the following done:

Prototyping (Figma)

User Form (JavaScript)
Homepage (Bootstrap)

UBC Engineering Physics' Robot Course 2020

plastic + hot glue + no sleep = robot summer (WFH edition)

Skills: C++, SolidWorks, Soldering

ENPH 253 (a.k.a the robot course) is best known by second year engineering physics students as hell a great learning experience. While past students will recall this summer as endless hours spent in the machine shop, electronics lab, and in class, due to the COVID-19 pandemic the class of 23' cohort will remember all the time spent at home. All jokes aside, this course will be one of my most memorable and unique experiences. From 7 hour+ zoom labs to soldering alone in my bedroom at midnight, I came away from this course with a new skill set in project management, firmware design, PCB manufacturing, and so much more! Here are my key takeaways: 

CAD Design

I was primarily in charge of mechanical design. Using SolidWorks, I created a virtual model of our robot with appropriate measurements, and parts designed from scratch. This design changed multiple times throughout the course of our project, but having a background in CAD design (from my design team work and co-op experience) made these adjustments easier. Our SolidWorks model was further used for manufacturing, getting weight/sizing, and adding electrical parts to our chassis. (See iteration here: https://tinyurl.com/y9fy2cvz)

Electronics 

Each member of our group was responsible for manufacturing PCBs for our servos, motors, sensors, and Blue Pill development board. Another member of my group created the electrical design using KiCAD, and everyone soldered circuits accordingly. 

Software

We all contributed to our robot's code and tailored it to our individual robot's set-up. We used C++ in Platform IO (VS Code), and interfaced with the BluePill board. A sample of our code can be found here: https://github.com/calistaa/robot2020

Tantalus - UBC Rocket Design Team

attempting launch student designed and manufactured experimental rockets to 30,000 feet

Skills: Project Management, SolidWorks, ANSYS Fluent 

Since day one at UBC, I have been a member of the rocket design team. From first-year member, to simulations/external structures specialist, to now a project manager, my years on this design team have taught me the most about engineering. 

CAD Design (SolidWorks)

I started on this software in my first-year of university, and I have become quite confident in my capabilities. Some notable projects include: a two foot tall model rocket, a 3 foot tall nose cone with segmented part (for feasibility on a 3D printer), a jig for sanding easier, and various rocket fins for simulations testing. 

Project Management

I am currently in my third-year as a member of the team, and my first-year of leading our project. Some of my favourite parts of this job are coordinating design changes between our 44 members, working with the other leads to plan budgeting and timelines, and leading team-wide integration meetings. I like setting goals with my team-mates and helping to reach them. 

ANSYS Fluent (Computational Fluid Dynamics)

As a member of the teams simulations/external structures sub group, I worked with ANSYS Fluent. I mainly used this program to run a few CFD simulations with our rocket's air frame to understand the drag profile.

Robotic Claw - APSC 101

kinda im"pasta"ble if you ask me :p

Skills: C (Arduino), Project Management

The autonomous claw was this first time I had ever built a robotic device from scratch. With the help of 5 other team mates (with no robotic experience) we were able to design, evaluate, and construct two claws within three weeks. The tasks of our two claws were to pick up dry pasta and metal blocks, I worked mainly on the latter.

C (Arduino)

Our system integrated a sonar sensor, servo, and Arduino board to complete this challenge. We ran into issues with hardware that were resolved after many visits to office hours. 

Project Management

Since we were a newly formed group, it was best to implement a structured plan that divided the tasks according to strength, and set a timeline for project deliverables. We also showcased our work with a tri-fold poster board that highlighted our design process, justifications, and implementation.
